Every day, people from all walks of life are seriously injured or killed due to the negligence of others. Personal injury law allows an injured person to file a civil lawsuit and receive compensation for losses stemming from a motor vehicle accident or any other incident where an injury occurs, such as a dog bite, or a “slip-and-fall” at the grocery store.

A successful personal injury case will result in payments to an injured party to compensate for medical expenses, pain & suffering, and lost wages.

Estate Planning refers to the process of anticipating and arranging how a person’s affairs will be handled in the event of their death or incapacitation. If you are over the age of 18 and of sound mind, you should be proactive about creating a will or a trust to ensure your assets are protected and passed down according to your plans.

Mediation is a form of alternative dispute resolution and a great way to resolve a conflict without the hassle and cost of a lengthy litigation process. It is a great tool for opposing parties to address the underlying issues involved in the dispute, avoid the stress and unpredictability of court, and have a say in the final agreement.

Our certified mediators use both mediation techniques and experience in the legal field to thoroughly analyze both sides of the conflict, identify the strengths and weaknesses of each party, and help reach a fair resolution.
