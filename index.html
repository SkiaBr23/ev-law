Every day, people from all walks of life are seriously injured or killed in motor vehicle accidents due to the negligence or recklessness of others. Personal injury law allows an injured person to file a civil lawsuit and receive a legal remedy for losses stemming from an accident or other incident where injury occurs, like an animal bite or a “slip-and-fall” at the grocery store.

A successful PI case may result in payments to an injured party to compensate for medical expenses, pain & suffering and wages lost as a result of death or incapacitation.

Estate planning refers to the process of anticipating and arranging how a person’s affairs will be handled in the event of his/her death or incapacitation. Estate planning reduces or even eliminates many of the costs and uncertainties over the administration of a probate, or the judicial process whereby a will is “proved” in court to be valid.

Although many people see creating a will as something only “old people” have to do, anyone over the age of 18 and of sound mind can and should create a will to eliminate these uncertainties and ensure your assets are passed down to the correct people. Having a will also helps prevent any family dispute that may turn up over a piece of property or other asset of the deceased.

Pursuant to Rhode Island law, a real estate buyer has the right to choose their own attorney to perform the title examination. This involves reviewing public records and examining the chain of title to ensure the seller actually owns the property and to figure out whether any third parties have a claim to the property you are looking to purchase.

Additionally, it is important to have an attorney to ensure the seller’s offer and the purchase and sales agreement adequately represent your needs and interests.

For example, the purchase of a home should be contingent upon a satisfactory home inspection. Buying a home is an exciting, but also a very stressful time and it is our goal to alleviate that stress to the best of our ability.

When working with the United States government to determine your status as a citizen, or the status of a loved one, it is always in your best interest to have an experienced immigration attorney to represent your through this difficult time.

Alien status, rights, duties and the process of obtaining citizenship are all complex legal matters and any misstep in these processes may have serious negative consequences for those trying to gain admission or citizenship.

Whether you are looking to obtain a green card, seeking asylum, or fighting deportation, we can help you navigate the immigration process and ensure things are done the RIGHT WAY!
